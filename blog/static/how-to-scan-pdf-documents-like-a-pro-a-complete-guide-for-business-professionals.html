What Does It Mean to Scan a PDF?

When you scan a PDF, you are converting a physical document into a digital format using a scanner or mobile app. The resulting PDF is usually an image-based file that contains a snapshot of the document.

Modern PDF scanners, however, can go further. They use OCR (Optical Character Recognition) to recognize text and turn your scanned document into a searchable and editable PDF. This makes it possible to highlight, copy, or extract information automatically.

Common Uses for Scanned PDFs

  • Archiving invoices, contracts, or receipts
  • Digitizing handwritten forms or reports
  • Creating searchable company records
  • Sending signed agreements electronically

Scanning PDFs is a crucial step for businesses that want to transition to paperless operations and unlock automation benefits.

Image-Based vs Searchable Scanned PDFs

Not all scanned PDFs are created equal. Understanding the difference between image-based and searchable PDFs is essential.

Type Description Example Use Case
Image-Based PDF A simple scan that captures an image of the page, without recognizing the text. Quick visual storage or reference
Searchable PDF A scan enhanced with OCR, allowing you to search and select text. Data extraction, indexing, or automation

Searchable PDFs are far more valuable for automation, as they can be processed by systems like TidiFul to extract structured data directly from the file.

How to Scan PDF Documents: Step-by-Step

Method 1: Using a Desktop Scanner

  1. Step 1: Place your document face down in the scanner.
  2. Step 2: Open your scanner software and choose "Scan to PDF."
  3. Step 3: Enable OCR or "Searchable PDF" mode if available.
  4. Step 4: Save your file with a clear name, such as Invoice_2025_01.pdf.

Pro Tip: Use 300 DPI resolution for crisp text and smaller file sizes.

Method 2: Using a Mobile App

Mobile scanning apps have made scan PDF workflows easier than ever. Tools like Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens, or CamScanner let you use your phone camera as a portable scanner.

  1. Step 1: Open the app and take a clear, flat photo of your document.
  2. Step 2: Crop and adjust lighting for legibility.
  3. Step 3: Choose "Save as PDF" and enable text recognition if possible.
  4. Step 4: Upload the scanned file to your preferred cloud storage or directly to TidiFul for data extraction.

Method 3: Using a Multifunction Printer

If your office has a multifunction printer, you can scan directly to PDF from its touchscreen interface.

  1. Step 1: Insert your pages into the feeder.
  2. Step 2: Select "Scan to Email" or "Scan to Folder" and choose the PDF format.
  3. Step 3: Enable OCR if the device supports it.
  4. Step 4: Review your scanned PDF for clarity and alignment.

Best Practices for Scanning PDFs

Follow these practices for high-quality scans and smooth automation:

  • Use good lighting and flat surfaces when scanning with a phone.
  • Enable OCR every time to make text searchable.
  • Avoid handwriting when possible or print clearly for better recognition.
  • Use consistent naming conventions for easy sorting and retrieval.
  • Batch scan related documents together to speed up processing.

Common Problems When Scanning PDFs (and How to Fix Them)

Problem Cause Solution
Blurry scans Low resolution or poor lighting Use at least 300 DPI and scan in a well-lit area
Huge file sizes High image quality or color scans Use grayscale or compress the PDF
Unreadable text No OCR enabled Turn on OCR or use TidiFul to detect text
Crooked pages Misaligned paper Use auto-crop and straighten features
